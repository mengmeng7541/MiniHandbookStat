§ Chapter 6 Categorical Data Analysis

Data: A r x c table 【Agresti (2002, p. 57) Job Satisfaction 】

The variables are income and job satisfaction, measured for the black males in a national (U.S.) sample. Both classifications are ordinal, job satisfaction with the categories very dissatisfied(VD), little dissatisfied(LD), moderately satisfied(MS), and very satisfied(VS).

Job Satisfaction
Very Dissatisfied Little Dissatisfied Moderately Satisfied Very Satisfied
< 15k 1 3 10 6
15k~25k 2 3 10 7
25k~40k 1 6 14 12
> 40k 0 1 9 11 
Job <- matrix(c(1,2,1,0, 3,3,6,1, 10,10,14,9, 6,7,12,11), 4, 4,
     dimnames = list(income=c("< 15k", "15k~25k", "25k~40k", "> 40k"),
     satisfaction=c("Very Dissatisfied", "Little Dissatisfied", "Moderately Satisfied", "Very Satisfied")))

fisher.test(Job, simulate.p.value=TRUE, B=1e5)
## 
##  Fisher's Exact Test for Count Data with simulated p-value (based
##  on 1e+05 replicates)
## 
## data:  Job
## p-value = 0.7804
## alternative hypothesis: two.sided

The p-value>0.05, so we can accept Null hypothesis, income and job satisfaction are independent.
